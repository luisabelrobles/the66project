Nutrition

Daily habit:

Lowering carb in-take, eating quality and nutrient-dense foods.

You are what you eat. Our gut health influences how our brain and body works independently and together. Delaying short-term urges to eat sugary foods. Focusing on the bigger picture; your mental and physical goals.

Benefits

Physical

Weight loss.

Veggies and fruits are lower in calories then processed foods. Eating healty diet free of processed foods help you maintain a low-calorie diet.

Fruits are high in sugar so eating too many fruits is not good for you. Eating leafy greens and plant-source fibers is ideal.

Healthy, beautiful skin.

Your eating habits influence all of your organs. Skin is the biggest organ in our bodies. Your skin reflects your inner-status and health.

To maintain healthy, thick, and moisturized skin, you need to have a diet rich omega-3 fatty acids. Omega-3 fats also reduce skin inflammation which casues acne.

Strong teeth and bones.

Getting your daily calcium and magnesium is essential for keeping your teeth and bones strong and healthy.

Healthy bones is important because as you age your bones deteriorate. Getting your daily requirements delays this process.

Improved gut health.

Our colon is full of natural/good bacteria. This influences our digestion and metabolism.

A diet rich in veggies, fruit, and legumes gives a good mix of prebiotics/probiotics which help that good bacteria thrive in our colon.

Muscle gain.

Exerising is part of the battle when it comes to muscle gain. If you are not fueling your body with the proper nutrients, your muscles cannot perform or recover efficiently.

Dietary proteins gives the body the neccesssary amino acids to rebuld muscle tissue after a good workout session.

Physiological (in other words your brain)

Higher energy levels.

Studies show that eating a balanced diet of unrefined carbs, protein, and fats correlates with having high levels of energy.

Focusing on veggies, fruits, and healhy fats in small protioned meals helps you avoid having an energy crash.

Fights depression.

Diets that are high in carbs (high glycemic load) can increase the symptoms of depression and feelings a fatigue.

The brain needs proper nutrition to function. Depriving your brain of essential vitamins, minerals, and fats raises the risk of depression.

Increased brain function.

As you age, your body loses function physically and mentally. By feeding you brain with the proper nutrients, you can maintain a healhy braina s you age.

Proper nutrition lowers oxidative stress in the brain. Having healthy blood flow relates to having a healthy brain.

Better sleep.

Obseity, drinking a lot of alcohol, and an diet high in processed foods cause airways to be repeatedly blocked with affects sleeping patterns.

A variety of whole foods with low-carb diet is helpful for people who have trouble falling asleep or maintaining good sleep.

Builds self-esteem.

Self-esstem usually start with body image. Having a positive body image comes from having a well-balanced diet.

Our minds and bodies are one entity. By feeding the body and brain properly, we can then have a more positive outlook on ourselves and those around us.
